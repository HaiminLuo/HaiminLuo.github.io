About

My research centers on 3D generation, with the goal of building controllable, structured, and high-fidelity generative models for visual content creation.

Within this agenda, I use neural rendering as a core modeling tool for geometry, appearance, and editable representations, while digital humans serve as a major application domain spanning hair, humans, motion, and embodied content.

I will obtain PhD degree in Computer Science at ShanghaiTech University in 2026, affiliated with the Visual and Data Intelligence Center (VDI) and the School of Information Science and Technology (SIST). I work with Prof. Jingyi Yu and also collaborate closely with Prof. Lan Xu.

Currently I am also fortunate to collaborate with Prof. Fernando De la Torre as a visiting scholar at the CMU Human Sensing Lab. Previously, I received my bachelor's degree in 2019 from Huazhong University of Science and Technology.

HairGPT: A Unified Autoregressive Framework for 3D Realistic Hairstyle Synthesis

Haimin Luo, Min Ouyang, Lan Xu, Jingyi Yu
April, 2026·SIGGRAPH 2026 (Journal Track, conditional acceptance)
HairGPT is a strand-centric framework that treats authentic strands as generative primitives and formulates 3D hair synthesis as a dual-decoupled autoregressive sequence modeling problem. By decoupling semantic scalp regions and hierarchical strand structure, it enables robust semantic conditioning, compositional editing, and high-fidelity synthesis of rare or complex hairstyles.
Project Page Video Cite

CGHair: Compact Gaussian Hair Reconstruction with Card Clustering

Haimin Luo, Srinjay Sarkar, Albert Mosella-Montoro, Francisco Vicente Carrasco, Fernando De la Torre
February, 2026·CVPR 2026
We present a compact pipeline for hair reconstruction from multi-view images. By clustering hair strands into representative cards and sharing Gaussian texture codebooks, CGHair achieves comparable visual quality with over 200x lower memory footprint and 4x faster strand reconstruction than prior approaches.
Project Page Video Cite

GaussianHair: Hair Modeling and Rendering with Light-aware Gaussians

Haimin Luo, Min Ouyang, Zijun Zhao, Suyi Jiang, Longwen Zhang, Qixuan Zhang, Wei Yang, Lan Xu†, Jingyi Yu†

NeuralDome: A Neural Modeling Pipeline on Multi-View Human-Object Interactions

Juze Zhang*, Haimin Luo*, Hongdi Yang, Xinru Xu, Qianyang Wu, Ye Shi, Jingyi Yu, Lan Xu, Jingya Wang
June, 2023·CVPR 2023
We construct a dense multi-view dome to acquire a complex human-object interaction dataset, named HODome, that consists of ~71M frames on 10 subjects interacting with 23 objects. To process the HODome dataset, we develop NeuralDome, a layer-wise neural processing pipeline tailored for accurate tracking, geometry reconstruction, and free-view rendering.
Paper Cite Code Dataset Video Project Page

NEPHELE: A Neural Platform for Highly Realistic Cloud Radiance Rendering

Haimin Luo, Siyuan Zhang, Fuqiang Zhao, Haotian Jing, Penghao Wang, Zhenxiao Yu, Dongxue Yan, Junran Ding, Boyuan Zhang, Qiang Hu, Shu Yin, Lan Xu, Jingyi Yu
March, 2023·arXiv 2023
In this paper, we resort to cloud rendering and present NEPHELE, a neural platform for highly realistic cloud radiance rendering. In stark contrast with existing neural rendering approaches, NEPHELE combines multiple remote GPUs and allows multiple people to view the same NeRF scene simultaneously.

Artemis: Articulated Neural Pets with Appearance and Motion Synthesis

Haimin Luo, Teng Xu, Yuheng Jiang, Chenglin Zhou, Qiwei Qiu, Yingliang Zhang, Wei Yang, Lan Xu, Jingyi Yu
February, 2022·ACM Transactions on Graphics (Proc. of SIGGRAPH 2022)
We present ARTEMIS, a novel neural modeling and rendering pipeline for generating articulated neural pets with appearance and motion synthesis. ARTEMIS enables interactive motion control, real-time animation, and photo-realistic rendering of furry animals.
